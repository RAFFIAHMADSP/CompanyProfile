Air vs Ocean Freight, Which One Should You Choose?

Air vs Ocean Freight, Which One Should You Choose?

September 12, 2024

As technological advancement continue in the international scale, we can see the continued developments for modern global economies as well through overseas manufacturing and heightened competitive rates for goods between countries as borders continue to thin in the increasingly interconnected world.

Read more: What Happens if a Country Fall Into Sovereign Default?

With increased competitors, businesses has started to find ways to better optimize their businesses further in hopes of finding a “leg up” from the rest of the competition. This is also due to the  increase in demands from international and global markets creating urgency for companies to develop effective and suitable ways in shipping their goods safely through borders.

Choosing the right mode of freight transport is a critical decision for businesses involved in global trade, especially when it comes to air and ocean freight as two of the most common types of transportation. Both options generally offer unique advantages and challenges, therefore further understanding the key factors to consider between them can help you make a more informed choice that better aligns with the shipping needs, budget, cargo, and timeline of your business.

In this guide, we'll explore the essential factors to consider when choosing between air and ocean freight, which generally includes the cost, speed, cargo type, environmental impact, and more of a certain freight transport.

Whether you're shipping urgent goods or bulky cargo, knowing the pros and cons of each mode can effectively optimize your logistics strategy and drive your business forward, creating further opportunities for its development.

Air Freight

Air Freight can be understood as the transportation of goods through an aircraft which generally utilizes both passenger and cargo dedicated aircraft in shipment transports. Air cargo can be classified into two groups which is between general cargo or special cargo where special cargo is designated for shipments that require special conditions or take additional care to move (sometimes requires certain temperatures or humidity controlled containers or special packaging).

Pros of Air Freight Transports:

  • Faster Delivery: It is highly time efficient compared to other forms of transport.Air freight is ideal for high-value and low-volume goods where speed is mportant especially for items that have limited shelf time
  • Quality Control: It is said that Air Freight transports are usually more safer or have higher security since it has stricter regulations and are more reliable in real time departures and arrival information.
  • Suitable for Light Cargo: Best for lightweight, high-value, perishable and fragile goods.

Cons of Air Freight Transports:

  • Higher Costs: Air freight is significantly more expensive than sea freight, especially for heavy or bulky items. Although it has lower transit times, additional charges may include fuel surcharges, handling fees, and airport charges.
  • Environmental Issue: Air Freight is known to have considerable carbon footprints in transportations

Ocean freight

Sea or Ocean Freight is the transportation of goods via ships and is widely used especially for international trade or shipments. Goods are generally transported into containers whether that be a Full Container Loads (FCL) or Less-than Container Loads (LCL) depending on whether the buyer wishes to purchase an entire or partial container.

Read more: Bagaimana Cara Mengekspor Bahan Baku Tekstil?

Pros of Sea Freight Transports:

  • Cheapers Costs: Sea freight is generally the most cost-effective option, especially for large, heavy, or bulky shipments. It allows for the transport of a high volume of goods at a lower per-unit cost**.**
  • Suitable for more heavier Cargo: Capable of handling all types of cargo, including oversized, heavy, and bulk goods. Fewer restrictions on cargo types and quantities.
  • More Eco-Friendly: Sea Freight is said to leave a relatively small carbon footprint in comparison to air freight

Cons of Sea Freight Transports:

  • Longer Transit Time: Significantly slower, with transit times ranging from weeks to over a month depending on the route. This makes it unsuitable for urgent shipments.
  • Higher Risks in Damage and Delays: risks include rough seas, port congestion, and increased handling during transit may lead to potential damage of goods, especially if not properly secured. It is also more common to experience delays in port between loading and unloading cargo.

Therefore, in choosing between air and sea freight, businesses should be adjusting it based on specific cargo needs, urgency, budget, and environmental considerations. Through the pros and cons between the two it can be seen that air freight is best for high-value, urgent, or lightweight goods, while sea freight excels with heavy, bulk, or non-urgent cargo, providing a cost-effective and sustainable solution.

Final Thoughts

With a more deeper understanding towards the pros and cons that lie between sea and air freight, we can further breakdown that factors that needs to be considered while choosing between the two transports are:

  • Costs: Businesses should carefully consider the most effective transport expense capable for their goods whether that be the costs of transports, handling or warehousing, and insurance.
  • Speed: This relates to the urgency of delivery, reliability in transit schedules or the goods itself (regular goods or perishables).
  • Cargo Type & Amount: Like in the pros and cons, businesses should adjust the freight transport according towards the mass or requirements surrounding the goods themselves as some may require specific packaging or handling needs.
  • Environmental Impact: As environmental issues continue to rise, it is better for businesses to also consider potential effects from certain freights transports.
  • Security: With different customs and regulations present in freights it is worth to check the restrictions or laws involving the transport of the certain goods and the services that is provided by transport to secure or track shipments.
