Why Are Goods Detained at Customs and How to Prevent Them?

Why Are Goods Detained at Customs and How to Prevent Them?

May 04, 2024

Receiving a notification that your package is stuck in customs can be frustrating. Whether you're the sender or the recipient, a customs detainment can throw a wrench in your plans and create significant delays. But why does this happen so often? Understanding the reasons behind these detentions can help you navigate the process more effectively and prevent future issues. Let's explore the primary causes of customs detainment and how to avoid them.

Common Reasons for Customs Detainment

Incomplete or Incorrect Paperwork

One of the leading causes of customs delays is incomplete or incorrect documentation. When shipping internationally, proper documentation is crucial to ensure that customs authorities have all the information they need to process your shipment. Common paperwork issues include:

  • Missing Forms: Essential forms like commercial invoices or customs declarations must accompany shipments. Missing documentation can halt the process entirely.
  • Incorrect Information: Errors in the recipient's address, item descriptions, or declared values can lead to delays.
  • Incomplete Details: Vague or missing information about the contents, such as product descriptions or tariff codes, can cause customs to hold a shipment for further inspection.

Unpaid Duties and Taxes

Import duties and taxes are a significant aspect of international shipping. Many countries impose these fees on shipments that exceed a certain value threshold. Common issues related to duties and taxes include:

  • Delivery Duty Unpaid (DDU): If the receiver is responsible for paying import duties and taxes upon delivery, any delay in payment can cause customs to hold the package.
  • Unclear Payment Responsibilities: Misunderstandings about who is responsible for paying these fees can lead to detainment.

Opting for Delivery Duty Paid (DDP) shipping, where the sender pays the necessary duties and taxes upfront, can often prevent such delays.

Prohibited or Restricted Items

Certain items are prohibited or restricted from entering specific countries. Shipping goods that fall into these categories can result in immediate detainment. Examples include:

  • Prohibited Items: These include explosives, narcotics, counterfeit goods, and other illegal items.
  • Restricted Items: Goods like alcohol, tobacco, perishables, and products made from endangered species may have specific import restrictions or require special permits.

To avoid issues, thoroughly research the destination country’s regulations before shipping.

Security Concerns and Random Inspections

Customs authorities conduct random inspections as part of their security measures. These inspections are designed to ensure that all imports comply with local laws and regulations. While these checks are often unavoidable, understanding the process can help:

  • Random Selection: Packages may be randomly selected for more thorough inspections, causing delays.
  • Content Verification: Customs officers may need to open packages to verify that the contents match the declared information.

How to Prevent Customs Detainment

While some causes of customs detainment are beyond your control, many can be mitigated with careful planning and attention to detail. Here are some strategies to reduce the risk of your goods being detained:

Provide Accurate and Complete Paperwork

Ensure that all required documents are accurate, complete, and legible. Essential paperwork includes:

  • Commercial Invoice: Clearly detail the sender’s and receiver’s information, item descriptions, and values.
  • Customs Declaration Forms: Accurately complete forms like CN22 or CN23.
  • Packing List: Include detailed descriptions of the contents and their quantities.

Pay Duties and Taxes in Advance

Whenever possible, opt for DDP shipping to handle duties and taxes upfront. This not only prevents delays but also simplifies the process for the receiver.

Know the Rules and Regulations

Familiarize yourself with the import regulations of the destination country. Ensure that your shipment complies with all relevant laws and restrictions. Avoid shipping prohibited items and adhere to restrictions on sensitive goods.

Partner with Reliable Shipping Providers

Choose experienced logistics providers who understand the complexities of international shipping. A good third-party logistics (3PL) provider can help you navigate customs regulations and ensure that your shipments comply with all requirements.

Choose Uniair Cargo for extensive and comprehensive logistics, we can help you end-to-end from first-mile to last-mile delivery. We are an AEO certified freight forwarder which means, the probability of goods being detained is close to zero.

Conclusion

Goods get detained at customs for various reasons, including incomplete paperwork, unpaid duties, security concerns, and shipping restricted items. By understanding these common causes and taking proactive steps to address them, you can minimize the risk of detainment and ensure smoother international shipping experiences.

Preparation and attention to detail are key. Ensure your paperwork is accurate and complete, pay necessary fees in advance, comply with all relevant regulations, and work with reliable shipping partners. With these measures in place, you can reduce the likelihood of your goods being detained at customs and enjoy more efficient global shipping.
